Rates, Credit, and Liquidity — Q1 2026 Market Context

This note supplements the main Q1 2026 market summary. Use it for questions about interest rates, bond markets, credit conditions, and liquidity in the first quarter of 2026.

Policy rates
The Federal Reserve, ECB, and Bank of England all left benchmark rates unchanged through Q1 2026. Market pricing implied shallow cuts later in 2026 contingent on continued disinflation, but officials emphasized data dependence.

Yield curves
Curve shapes were flatter than in 2025. Short-dated paper was anchored by policy, while long-end yields reacted to term premium and fiscal supply debate. Advisors discussing “rates market highlights” for Q1 2026 should note range-bound long yields rather than a sustained bear steepener.

Credit
Investment-grade issuance was orderly; non-financial corporates in technology and healthcare dominated new supply. Loan markets saw tighter spreads in software LBO paper but wider spreads in cyclical issuers tied to manufacturing PMIs. Structured credit (CLO mezzanine) saw stable demand from institutional buyers.

Liquidity
Money-market funds held elevated balances. Repo markets functioned normally aside from quarter-end window dressing. No systemic stress events were recorded in Q1 2026.
