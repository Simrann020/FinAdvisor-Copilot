Market Summary — Q1 2026 (Global Highlights)

This document is the primary internal market commentary for advisor briefings. It answers questions about Q1 2026 market highlights, first-quarter performance, macro trends, and cross-asset themes for the period ending 31 March 2026.

Equities — broad themes
In Q1 2026, global equities delivered positive but uneven returns. The S&P 500 gained approximately 4.2% in USD terms, led by large-cap growth and semiconductors. The NASDAQ-100 outperformed the broader US market as mega-cap technology names benefitted from resilient earnings and AI-related capex narratives. European equities (MSCI Europe) rose modestly in local currency; strength in luxury goods and industrials offset weakness in select banks. Japanese equities (Topix) were supported by corporate governance improvements and continued share buybacks. Emerging markets remained volatile: LatAm outperformed Asia ex-Japan on commodity exposure, while China-related benchmarks lagged on property-sector overhang and policy uncertainty.

Fixed income and rates
Central banks largely held policy rates steady in Q1 2026 after the late-2025 tightening cycle. Government bond curves were range-bound; the US 10-year yield oscillated within a narrow band as inflation prints softened gradually. Investment-grade credit spreads tightened slightly on strong corporate fundamentals. High-yield spreads were wider in energy sub-sectors tied to commodity price swings. Municipals saw steady demand from tax-sensitive investors.

Currencies and commodities
The US dollar index was mixed: firm against some G10 pairs on relative growth differentials, softer where local central banks surprised hawkishly. Gold rose on real-rate stability and periodic geopolitical hedging demand. Oil was volatile with supply disruptions in select regions partially offset by inventory builds. Industrial metals reflected uneven manufacturing PMIs globally.

Sector and style rotation
Within equities, information technology and communication services outperformed in Q1 2026. Energy was volatile but finished the quarter slightly positive. Utilities and consumer staples underperformed as investors favoured cyclical exposure early in the quarter before rotating partially back to quality factors into quarter-end.

Risks called out for advisor conversations
Key risks entering Q2 2026 included: (1) stickiness of core services inflation in major economies, (2) commercial real estate refinancing walls in several cities, (3) election-cycle policy uncertainty in multiple jurisdictions, and (4) geopolitical flare-ups affecting trade routes and energy supply.

Bottom line for client discussions
Q1 2026 market highlights can be summarized as: positive developed-market equities with US large-cap growth leadership, resilient but range-bound fixed income, a volatile EM complex, and mixed commodity performance. Positioning conversations should stress diversification, liquidity, and alignment with each client’s stated risk tolerance rather than tactical calls.
